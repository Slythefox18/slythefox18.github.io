Background Information


Garlic Mustard was brought to Canada as an edible plant in the early 1800s (Ontario's Invading Species Awareness Program, n.d.). This was because is was high in vitamin A and vitamin C and smells similar to garlic (Ontario's Invading Species Awareness Program, n.d.). As an example, the plant contains more vitamin C per weight than oragnes (Government of Ontario, 2023). Since then, the plant has spread accross much of North America
